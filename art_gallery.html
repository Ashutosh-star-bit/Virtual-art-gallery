Artwork 1: Abstract Sunrise


Click here for more info

This piece captures the energy of a new morning through pure, uncontrolled color.
The artist, Rina Sharma, moved away from traditional landscapes to focus on emotional expression.
It was originally titled “Morning Star” but was changed to reflect the true dynamic motion of the sun.

Materials Used for Abstract Sunrise

  • Heavy-duty canvas (300 GSM)
  • Acrylic paints (primary colors only)
  • Palette knives and sponges for texture
  • Finish: Matte varnish

Artwork 4: Zen Garden Photograph


Click here for more info

A simple, yet profound, photograph capturing the tranquility of a traditional Japanese garden.
The image emphasizes texture and pattern, encouraging the viewer to pause and reflect.
It is a powerful reminder that **``**simplicity**``** often holds the greatest beauty.

DIY: Steps to Create Your Own Abstract Art

  1. Gather your tools: canvas, acrylics, and brushes.
  2. Prepare your workspace with protective coverings.
  3. Apply a base layer and allow it to dry completely.
  4. Layer colors using dynamic, sweeping motions.
  5. Step back and admire your new creation!
